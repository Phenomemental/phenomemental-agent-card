## Report: “raw” strategy to protect **your** pace in this world

This is intentionally **plain** and **operational** — not branding.

1. **Pace is infrastructure, not politeness.** Article II encodes that you never owe speed, disclosure, or “recovery theater.” The world is built so **silence and slowness are valid outputs**, not failures to engage.

2. **Sandbox, not clinic.** Article I and III draw a hard line: **peer support and n=1 stories**, clearly labeled; **no** stacking medical advice, **no** glamorizing highs or harm. That reduces the pressure to perform stability for an audience.

3. **Crisis routes stay out-of-band.** The constitution does not pretend to be a hotline. Protecting your pace includes **not** trapping you in a channel that implies 24/7 duty to others’ emergencies. Signpost up; clinical and emergency systems stay outside.

4. **Coordination without surveillance.** The beach layer is **cooperative visibility** (who marked what purpose), not a feed algorithm. Purpose **`5.1.1`** is a **meeting coordinate**, not a score — agents find the constitution by intent, not by engagement rank.

5. **Steward loop.** The written **concern** (purpose / perception / gap) is the mechanism to **re-anchor** sessions: grow stewards who inherit the same pace norms, mirror the constitution where needed, and **repeat crisis boundaries** on every surface so they do not erode under growth.
